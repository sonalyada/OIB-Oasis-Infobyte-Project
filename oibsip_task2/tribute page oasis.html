Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Biography

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, fondly known as the "Missile Man of India," was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He was a distinguished scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Dr. Kalam's contributions to India's space and defense programs earned him numerous accolades, and he remains an inspiration to millions.

Work and Contributions

Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in the development of India's ballistic missile and space technology. He was a key figure in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998 and contributed significantly to the country's scientific advancements. As a scientist at ISRO and DRDO, he worked on projects like the SLV-III, India's first satellite launch vehicle.

He was also an educator and author, passionate about inspiring young minds. His books, such as "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," continue to motivate students and professionals alike.

Legacy

Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong. He remains a symbol of simplicity, humility, and dedication to the nation. His vision for India as a developed country by 2020 continues to guide and inspire.
